TrueSpirit

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

Objective


Provide a complete website design, information architecture, and navigation schema for a new school uniform retailer.


Challenge

TrueSpirit is a new internet retailer for schools and parents who want a modern school uniform for K-12 children. Their target customers are school administrators, parents, and students. Some site users will be coming into the website with a school-specific code, while others are just browsing the entire catalog. The challenge was to make the site easily navigable by both types of users, while maintaining the feel of TrueSpirit’s brand.


Process


Research & Discovery

User Research | User Interviews | Comparative Analysis | Competitive Analysis

During the kickoff meeting, I was able to gather information about the project, intended users, and business objectives.



Information Architecture

Card Sorting | Site Maps | User Flows

Using the information I gathered during the research and discovery step, I applied the card sorting methodology to users to determine the best way to organize the items on the site and create user flows.



Wireframing & Prototying

Sketching | Omnigraffle | InVision

After the creating the information architecture, I began the ideation process to sketch and create wireframes. Once initial wireframes were created, I used InVision to create a prototype to test on users.



User Testing

Individual User Testing | Iterations

The prototype was tested on multiple users and changes were applied in the next iteration based on the input gathered.



User Testing
